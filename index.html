The Problem
Auction packs are
deliberately complex

Sellers and auctioneers are not obligated to make legal packs easy to understand. The risk falls entirely on you.

😰

You have days, not weeks

Legal packs drop 2–5 days before auction. A full solicitor review costs £500–£1,500 and takes time most investors don't have.

📋

Hundreds of pages, buried traps

A typical pack is 80–300 pages. The clause that costs you £8,000 is on page 247 of the Special Conditions — not the summary.

🔒

No recourse after the hammer

Auction purchases are legally binding on the day. Discovering a short lease or missing certificate after bidding means you're committed regardless.

How It Works
From confusing clause
to plain English — instantly

We don't just summarise. We translate every clause into what it actually means for your money.

📄 Original Document Special Conditions · Clause G15
The Buyer shall be bound by and shall
comply with all matters registered at
the Land Registry, whether or not the
same are disclosed or referred to in this
agreement, and shall indemnify the Seller
against all liability arising therefrom,
whether known or unknown at the date
of this agreement.
🤖 LegalPack AI Translation
⚠️ Unlimited Unknown Liability Clause
You are agreeing to take responsibility for all legal obligations attached to this property — even ones not disclosed in the pack and unknown to anyone at time of sale.
⚡ Buyer Impact: Planning enforcement, unregistered rights of way, drainage disputes — all become your problem at completion.
