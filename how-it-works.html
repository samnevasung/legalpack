How AI Auction Legal Pack Analysis Works

Upload your legal pack ZIP or PDF — LegalPack AI reads every page like a cautious UK property solicitor and delivers a structured risk report with flagged issues, plain English explanations, and exact clause references in 3–4 minutes.

The three-step process

Most auction buyers never read their legal pack in full — not because they don't want to, but because packs are 80–300 pages of dense legal language and the auction is days away. LegalPack AI solves this in three steps.

📤

Step 1 — Upload your legal pack

Drop in the ZIP file you received from the auctioneer, individual PDFs, or multiple files at once. LegalPack AI handles any format and any size — including large bundles of 30 or more documents that exceed 50MB. There is no file size cap.

🤖

Step 2 — AI reads every single page

The AI reads every document in full — title register, title plan, special conditions of sale, local authority search, drainage and environmental searches, the full lease, property information forms, and all attachments. It cross-references between documents to catch inconsistencies (for example, a building regulation certificate referenced in the TA6 but missing from the pack).

📊

Step 3 — Receive your plain English risk report

You receive a full structured report: an investor verdict (buy / caution / avoid), a list of risk flags sorted by severity, confirmed costs extracted from the documents, a mortgageability assessment, and a personalised before-bidding checklist. Every finding links to the exact source document and clause.

⏱ Typical turnaround

Most legal packs are processed in 3–4 minutes. Very large packs with 30+ documents may take up to 8 minutes. You will see a live progress indicator while the analysis runs.

What gets analysed in your legal pack

LegalPack AI reads every document type typically found in a UK auction legal pack and applies a specific set of checks to each one.

Special Conditions of Sale

The most important — and most overlooked — document in any auction pack. The AI scans for hidden buyer costs (seller legal fees, administration charges, VAT), shortened completion periods, late completion interest rates, indemnity obligations, overage clauses, and any condition that overrides the standard Common Auction Conditions.

Title Register and Title Plan

Checks tenure (freehold or leasehold), title guarantee level, any charges or restrictions registered against the title, possessory vs absolute title, and whether the registered owner matches the seller. Flags lender sales, missing title numbers, and boundary anomalies.

Searches (Local Authority, Drainage, Environmental)

Extracts road adoption status, planning enforcement notices, tree preservation orders, compulsory purchase orders, listed building status, contaminated land findings, flood risk, and public sewer locations. Each finding is presented with its specific risk implication for the buyer.

Lease (leasehold properties)

Calculates the remaining lease length, identifies ground rent amounts and review mechanisms, extracts service charge estimates, flags restrictive covenants in the lease (no pets, no subletting, no alterations without consent), and identifies any doubling or high-cap ground rent clauses that could affect mortgageability.

Missing documents

Identifies documents referenced in the pack but not provided — building regulations certificates, guarantees, planning permissions — and explains the specific risk each missing document creates for the buyer.

What the risk report contains

The report is structured to give you everything you need to make a go/no-go decision before the auction, without requiring you to understand legal terminology.

  • Investor verdict — a single headline assessment: standard purchase, proceed with caution, or serious concerns
  • Risk flags by severity — critical, warning, and informational flags, each with the exact source clause
  • Confirmed costs — every financial obligation expressly stated in the documents (seller fees, deposits, penalties), with no guesswork
  • Mortgageability assessment — standard mortgage, bridging finance, or cash buyers only — and why
  • Missing document list — every document absent from the pack and the risk it creates
  • Before-bidding checklist — a personalised list of what still needs verifying before you bid
